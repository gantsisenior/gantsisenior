GENERAL INFORMATION

1. Names and Opening

The official name of the school is Ghanzi Senior Secondary School. It was opened in 1994 and had to initially operate from Ledumang Senior Secondary School in Gaborone because construction at its permanent in Ghanzi had just begun. The school moved to Ghanzi at the beginning of 1995 when some buildings had been completed.

2. How To Get Here

Ghanzi Senior Secondary School is situated behind the Ghanzi Brigade Centre off the Maun-Ghanzi road that passes in front of the latter. The school is accessible by three routes: Gaborone-Ghanzi(685km), Maun-Ghanzi(286km) and Mamuno-Ghanzi(199km). We are happy to inform you that all these roads are completely tarred. There is currently adequate public transport between Ghanzi and other major centres, particularly between Gaborone and Ghanzi with three modern buses that go either way every day of the week. There is also public transport (combi/taxi) to school from the bus rank.

3. Ownership

Ghanzi Senior Secondary School is a wholly government-owned school with an enrolment of around 700 students per year.

4. Admission

Admission at school is subject to the student having satisfied the conditions generally applicable to all Government Senior Secondary Schools as outlined in the Secondary School Regulations of 1989. The maximum age of admission into form 4 is 22 years. If a student is more than 22 years on or before 1st March of the year of admission, one cannot be admitted. Prospective candidates into Form 4 must have successfully completed the Botswana Junior Certificate course or its equivalent. It should noted though that those with equivalent qualifications from recognized educational institutions might also be considered for admissions, subject to approval by the Permanent Secretary, Ministry of Education. All students are expected to produce proof of date of birth, either a Birth Certificate, Passport or National Identity Card(Omang).

5. Co-curricular Activities

Co-curricular activities are designed to offer change from the routine of the classroom and to enrich the overall lives of the students. To date such activities include sports: Football(soccer), Netball, Volleyball, Rugby, Softball, Boxing, Karate, Badminton, Table-Tennis, Basketball and Tennis. Athletics include events such as track: flat races and long-distance races, and field: Discuss, Javelin, Short-Put and High, Long and Triple Jumps. All students are expected to participate in ball sports and/or athletics. Every student should take part in at least one sporting code. For those who cannot for medical reasons will be required to produce some form of evidence, preferably from a recognized medical practitioner to support their position.

6. Clubs and Associations

All clubs and associations form part of the Sports and Recreation Department. All clubs and associations currently offered in school:
PACT, Health Club, Business Studies Association, Home Economics Club, AIDS Committee, Mathematics ans Science Club, History Society, Environmental Awareness Club, Ballroom Dance, Drama Club, Scout, Chess Club, Press Club, Debating Club, Junior Achievement Botswana, Band, Agricultural Club, Crime Prevention Club, Art Club, Scripture Union, Poetry Club, Abstinence Club, Botswana Works-Camps, Road Safety Club and Traditional Dance.

All clubs have staff patrons. Where necessary persons from the community are allowed to help students in practice and training provided they maintain professional relationships with the students. These people will be supervised by Senior Teacher I Sports and Recreation. He/She will be assisted by club patrons and coaches.

7. Uniform

The wearing of uniform is compulsory for both boys and girls. Uniform is bought directly from the Supplier and the school does not keep any uniform to sell to students. All uniform items must conform to the school colour and pattern. Uniform prices vary from year to year and the school does not have control over price fluctuations.

8. Dining Hall

Provided the right furniture is used, students should be able to feed in only two shifts, that is 650 students per shift. The hall itself is large enough to accommodate 800 students in any one sitting. Students are required to have all their meals in the Dining Hall. Only tea break is served outside the hall.

top

9. Boarding

The school has boarding facilities, but students may choose to stay outside campus. Boys and girls are under the supervision of the Boarding Master and Boarding Matron respectively. Boarding students are expected to abide by the rules and regulations laid down and to extend to the boarding authorities, the respect and obedience due to parent/guardian. Parents must ensure that their children have necessary items to be used in the hostels.

NB: Boarding is a privilege, not a right and as such any student who fails to abide by the rules will straight away lose it.

top

10. Health Care

Students who fall sick are normally permitted to visit Ghanzi Primary Hospital for medical attention. The boarding department keeps a First Aid Kit to help the students' minor injuries. Unless the condition is critical, students are allowed to visit the Hospital during afternoon study. Students are advised to always have enough funds to pay for medical help when called upon to do so. The medical fee is P5.00 for locals and P30.00 for expatriates. Batswana aged above 16 years are to produce Omang to be assisted at clinics or hospitals.

11. The Prefect System/Student Representative Council(SRC)

SRC members are elected from the student body by the students to help in the day-to-day running of the school. The SRC's duties are supervisory in nature and include supervision in boarding, around the academic area, in the dining hall and anywhere their supervision is needed. All students are expected to cooperate with the SRC and take reasonable instructions from them. It must must be recognized that SRC play an important role in making school life comfortable for the students. Therefore failure to cooperate with and obey reasonable instructions from them will result in punishment.

12. Student Identity (ID) Card

Students shall have Identity cards and the card costs P30.00(subject to change). The ID helps identify students in and out of the school. They also use them to borrow books at the school library. Therefore Students are advised to carry their ID cards all the time.

13. Student Welfare

Parents are encouraged to open savings accounts for their children at the post office and banks for safe keeping of their money. They are also encouraged to do Omangs for their children or passports in case of under 16 children.

14. School Fees

NON-CITIZEN STUDENTS

Kindly be informed that all non-citizen students (i.e. non-Batswana students) pay school fees of P6000 per year which can be paid in term instalments of P2000. Term instalments must be paid within the first 10 days of the term. Non-payment will lead to the student being sent home to collect the fees. If the student is boarding, they will pay an additional P700 per year which, like school fees, can be paid as per term.

CITIZEN STUDENTS

Since January 2006, Government has implemented cost sharing of 5% of the total cost of education per student. At senior school level, it costs P9000 per year and P450 is the 5%.

Level of education Plan 1: Full payment of cost sharing Plan 2: One payment per term Plan 3: Two payments per term
Senior Secondary P450 P150 P75 x2

Plan 1 - Shall be paid by the end of the first week of the term
Plan 2 - Shall be paid by the end of the first week of each term
Plan 3 - Shall be paid by the end of the second week of the term

NB: Parents are however encouraged to pay during the school holidays to avoid congestion at the beginning of the term.

VERY IMPORTANT: Parents/guardians shall choose one plan of payment (from above) and enter into a Contractual Agreement with the school on the terms of payment. The Contractual Agreement will be legally binding and may be used for litigation where a parent defaults in payment.

Payments shall be made in cash, bank guaranteed cheques, telegraphic transfers and postal orders whereupon official receipts will be issued.

15. Parents Teachers Association (PTA)

All parents whose children have been admitted at this school are automatically members of the PTA and are expected to attend PTA meetings, the same goes for all teachers. PTA provides a forum for keeping the school and the community in close contact and ensures parental involvement. The governments expects parents to contribute to their children's education. The association has an Executive Committee which comprises of Chairperson, Secretary, Treasurer and additional members.

PTA FUNDS

a) PTA Development Fund: Every student is to pay P120 as a PTA fund at the beginning of the year. This will help the PTA to fund some activities such as Open Day and Prize Giving day. Open Day, which started in 2006, is a day on which parents/guardians and teachers discuss the students' academic performance and the general welfare. Parents are encouraged to attend. It is done every second Friday on each term. The PTA also holds a meeting on this day.

b) TOILET LEVY: Every student is expected to pay P20 per term of P60 per annum. The proceeds are used to hire casual employees to clean toilets on behalf of the students.
