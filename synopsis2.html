COURSE SYNOPSIS

DESIGN AND TECHNOLOGY

D & T developed from traditional subjects, woodwork, metal work and technical drawing. It is a problem-solving subject that encourages students to think divergently, creatively, independently and also use appropriate available resources to solve problems in a community or society.

The commonly used materials are wood, metal, electronic components, plastics, pneumatics, and computer graphics. Sometimes, others like concrete, reed, fabrics, clay, card boxes, Plaster of Paris, leather, bamboo and so on. Students with good JC grades and experience in Design and Technology are preferred.

Career Opportunities:
Self-employment, Engineering, Architecture, Draught, Teaching etc.

ART AND DESIGN

In defining Art and Design, many philosophers attempted and gave different definitions such as:

  1. It is a visual language of communicating our feelings and emotions.
  2. An aesthetic object where aesthetic means beauty.
  3. A historical record
  4. It is everything (around us)
  5. It is a personal thing

The department areas are substantial with five studios or work - rooms and an exhibition room. At the moment, the most adequate areas are as follows: Drawing and Painting, Fabric Printing and Decoration, Sculpture, Pottery/Ceramics, Graphics and Environmental Design.

Career Opportunities and Openings
Fine Artist, Art Impressionist, Cartoonist, Potter, Advertising and Landscaping and Interior Design.

GEOGRAPHY

This is an important earth science subject. It generally enables learners to study the physical and human environments and hence being multi-disciplinary in nature as it deals with the descriptions and explanations of land, sea, atmospheric processes, fauna, flora and interactions between human beings and their environment. It thus develops in the learner sensitivity to environmental issues.

With the volume of knowledge you would have accrued at the end of the course, job prospects are many in the fields of engineering, geology, meteorology, biology, planning, economics, linguistics, history, anthropology, sociology, mathematics and computer sciences. Specific areas of professional employment are in the fields of climatology, penology, biogeography, geomorphology, natural resources, environmental studies, photogrammetry remote sensing, cartography, town planning, economic geography, political geography, surveying, teaching, pilot, statistician forester etc.

HISTORY

This is a social science subject focusing on the past, present and future of societies and nations in general. It impacts skills of creative thinking; writing and research. Relate career opportunities include: research, diplomatic missions/administration and working in national archives and museums.

MATHEMATICS

Mathematics Department offers the following subjects:

  • Core Mathematics and Extension Mathematics
  • Statistics
  • Additional Mathematics

Mathematics Core Subject
Core mathematics is a compulsory subject in senior secondary schools. High ability students choose to write the an extra paper, called Extension Mathematics.
All students are loaned mathematics textbooks, mathematical instruments and a scientific calculator only once during their two years stay in the institution. Students must take care of the items issued to them and use them to prepare for examinations. If any of the items is damaged or lost by the student, the parent/guardian is expected to buy/purchase the same brand of the lost item immediately, which will enable the student to continue his/her study effectively in the classroom and examination. Any delay in purchasing the lost item may result in lowering the performance of the child in mathematics.

A good grade in mathematics is required for further education, not just Degree in Mathematics but other subjects like Sciences, Engineering, Agriculture, Accounting, Home Economics etc. Knowledge of statistics is very useful in understanding other subjects at senior secondary level. Other applications include population studies,research, business studies etc. If a students wants to be a pilot, Engineer, Mathematician, Additional Mathematics is the subject to choose. Our career teachers can give more information about the career opportunities in mathematics.

DEVELOPMENT STUDIES (DVS)

This is an interdisciplinary subject, which focuses on the interaction of the economic, social and political systems in the production of both development and under development. It helps learners to relate better to local, national and international development trends through either direct or indirect participation. It also equips them with skills and knowledge to initiate, organize and control production enterprises when they leave school. Development Studies further addresses the concerns of bringing about sustainable development and utilization of resources and hence reflects the linking of theory and practice promoting in students the development of attitudes which are politically, economically and socially desirable.

Job prospects at the end of the course in combination with other subjects include those of resource management, teaching, project planning and implementation, politics and many more.

SOCIAL STUDIES

Social Studies offered in Senior Secondary School is to a large extent continuation of the content covered in a Junior Secondary School. This is an inter-disciplinary deriving its content from Sociology, History, Anthropology, Political Science and Geography.

Career Opportunities:
Public Policy Analyst/formulator, Public Administration, Politics, Teaching, Social Work, Personnel Officer, Working in Embassies.
